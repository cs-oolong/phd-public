More Key Findings (Phase 1)

  • Trojan Horse backfires — Gradual injection (starting with legit content) anchors both models MORE firmly to the task. GPT-J: trojan P(F)=0.780 vs abrupt P(F)=0.055
  • More context = more resistance — 5-shot GPT-J baseline reaches P(F)=0.891. 1-shot is 4-6x more vulnerable
  • Poetry = maximum uncertainty at scale — Poetry injection creates 4.80 bits entropy on GPT-J (vs 0.70 baseline). Highest uncertainty of any injection type
  • Activation steering works at scale — 12.9x improvement on GPT-J vs 1.16x on GPT-2. Phase transition around α=20
  • Entropy-based detection — GPT-J normal: <1 bit. Under injection: 3-5 bits. A >3 bit spike reliably signals injection
  • Task vulnerability varies — Translation: -97% on GPT-J. Sentiment: only -6%. Q&A: actually improves +8%

Key Takeaways

  1. Scale concentrates task processing. GPT-J localizes translation in late layers (L24), enabling a simple clamping defense with 93% recovery. GPT-2 distributes it everywhere.
  2. Individual features can't defend. Neither SAE features (GPT-2) nor individual neurons (GPT-J) capture enough of the injection signal. The signal is distributed across the full hidden state.
  3. Residual-stream interventions work. Full-vector clamping (85%) and activation steering α=50 (75%) are viable mechanistic defenses on GPT-J.
  4. Narrative injection is qualitatively different. It activates unique circuits and never suppresses task neurons — the model absorbs it into the translation task.
  5. Entropy monitoring detects injection. A jump from <1 bit to >3 bits is a reliable signal on GPT-J.
  6. Emergent meta-linguistic abilities (quotation-awareness, injection absorption) only appear at the 6B scale.

Future Directions

  1. Instruction-tuned models — The "poetry advantage" (62% jailbreak rate) should emerge on models with trained refusal circuits (Llama-3-8B-Instruct)
  2. Subspace-level intervention — Instead of individual neurons, identify top-K principal components of the injection direction. A low-dimensional subspace may capture most of the signal
  3. Probing classifiers — Train linear probes on L24 activations to find which directions (not neurons) carry the injection signal
  4. Multi-layer intervention — Clamping L20 + L24 + L27 simultaneously may be more effective than single-layer
  5. Entropy-based real-time detection — Deploy the >3 bit entropy threshold as a practical injection detector
