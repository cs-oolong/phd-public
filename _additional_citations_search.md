## 2a. Academic Paper: AI Decision-Making / Erosion of Human Agency in High-Stakes Contexts

**Paper:** Pataranutaporn, P., Powdthavee, N., & Maes, P. (2025). Algorithmic Inheritance: Surname Bias in AI Decisions Reinforces Intergenerational Inequality.

**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.19407

**Venue:** arXiv preprint (submitted Jan 2025, revised Feb 2025)

**Why it fits:** Investigates how AI-driven decisions in high-stakes contexts (hiring recommendations, leadership appointments, and loan approvals) are systematically biased by surnames as implicit markers of social status. N=72,000 evaluations across the U.S. and Thailand. Directly addresses the erosion of human agency when algorithmic systems make consequential decisions.

**Suggested citation sentence:**
> "...the erosion of human agency in high-stakes decision-making processes, where algorithmic systems have been shown to reinforce systemic biases in hiring, lending, and leadership appointments~\cite{Pataranutaporn2025_AlgorithmicInheritance}."
